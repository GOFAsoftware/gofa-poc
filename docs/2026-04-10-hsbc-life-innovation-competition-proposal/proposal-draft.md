# Draft Proposal

## Proposal Title

HSBC Life ActiveShield: Building a Health & Wellness Ecosystem That Turns Protection Into Everyday Prevention

## Category

Health & Wellness Ecosystem

## Executive Summary

HSBC Life has an opportunity to evolve from a provider that customers mainly encounter at purchase, renewal, or claims, into a trusted everyday partner in health and wellbeing. We propose HSBC Life ActiveShield, a digital wellness ecosystem that combines preventive health screening, simple risk stratification, personalised movement and healthy-ageing support, and insurer-facing population insight.

The solution is designed to help HSBC Life move beyond claims payment and become more relevant in customers' daily lives. It focuses especially on early detection of mobility decline, healthy-ageing support, and behaviour change through digital engagement. By combining short digital assessments, ongoing wellness journeys, and portfolio-level insight, HSBC Life can improve customer value, strengthen retention, and create a differentiated proposition in the health and wellness space.

## The Problem

Insurance customers often interact with insurers too infrequently. In between purchase and claims, the relationship can feel passive and low value. At the same time, many customers, especially older adults and families with caregiving responsibilities, face rising pressure from healthcare costs, mobility decline, frailty risk, and chronic-condition management.

Current wellness offers in the market are often too generic. They may provide articles, rewards, or disconnected app features, but they do not build a strong loop between:
- identifying member need
- recommending an intervention
- sustaining engagement
- measuring progress
- generating actionable business insight

This creates a gap for both customer and insurer. Customers do not get timely, relevant support. Insurers miss the chance to drive prevention, improve engagement, and shape smarter propositions using real behavioural signals.

## Our Proposal

HSBC Life ActiveShield is a prevention-led health and wellness ecosystem with four connected layers.

### 1. Preventive Screening

Members begin with a short, accessible digital assessment experience. This includes:
- simple onboarding questions on lifestyle and health risk factors
- mobility and balance screening to identify early movement decline
- healthy-ageing indicators relevant to older adults
- optional future integration with wearable and health-app data

This provides an easy entry point that is more actionable than generic content and more scalable than high-touch manual review alone.

### 2. Risk Stratification

After screening, the platform classifies members into practical support tiers. Rather than producing complex medical language, the system translates results into plain-language insights such as:
- maintain current health momentum
- build strength and stability
- start guided low-risk movement support
- follow up proactively for higher-risk needs

This helps HSBC Life identify who may benefit from early support while also making the output understandable for customers.

### 3. Personalised Intervention

Based on the member's profile, the platform recommends an appropriate next step, for example:
- short AI-guided movement sessions
- healthy-ageing exercise plans
- accessibility-first routines for older adults
- progression-based rehabilitation style plans for users with higher movement risk
- engagement prompts and follow-up reminders

This shifts the insurer relationship from reactive protection to proactive support.

### 4. Insurer Insight Layer

At portfolio level, HSBC Life receives aggregate views such as:
- assessment coverage
- risk mix across linked members
- higher-risk cohort requiring active support
- engagement performance across movement and learning modules
- intervention uptake by member segment

These insights can inform proposition design, partnership strategy, campaign targeting, and future product development.

## Why This Is Different

This proposal does not treat wellness as an add-on. It creates an operating model where prevention, engagement, and insight reinforce each other.

Traditional model:
- customer buys policy
- insurer communicates occasionally
- support is activated late

ActiveShield model:
- customer screens early
- insurer identifies needs sooner
- customer enters a personalised support journey
- engagement generates measurable outcomes and insight

## Use Of Technology

Technology is used in a practical and customer-centred way:
- digital onboarding and assessment
- AI-guided movement and personalised intervention logic
- optional responsible integration of wearable and health-app data
- aggregate analytics for insurer decision-making

The objective is not to use technology for novelty alone. It is to make prevention scalable, engaging, and measurable.

## Customer Centricity

The solution is designed for real-life usability:
- short and intuitive onboarding
- plain-language outputs
- older-adult friendly interaction design
- low-friction movement support
- different pathways for different risk and readiness levels

This matters because the target users include not only digitally confident adults but also customers with lower technology confidence or higher health vulnerability.

## ESG And Sustainability Relevance

The proposal supports social sustainability by:
- improving access to preventive health support
- supporting healthy ageing
- reducing exclusion of older users through accessible design
- encouraging earlier and lower-cost intervention before more severe decline

It also supports a more sustainable insurer-customer relationship by building long-term preventive value rather than relying only on post-event interaction.

## Implementation Plan

### Phase 1: Pilot
- Launch with a focused member segment such as healthy-ageing customers or policyholders interested in wellness benefits.
- Offer digital screening and a limited set of guided movement journeys.
- Position GOFA as the enabling health-engagement partner while HSBC Life owns the programme brand and member proposition.

### Phase 2: Expand Engagement
- Add personalised reminders, family engagement, and richer intervention pathways.
- Introduce partner offers in fitness, nutrition, or health coaching.
- Add reward logic, campaign mechanics, and broader member-value packaging.

### Phase 3: Portfolio Intelligence
- Use aggregate insight to refine proposition design, customer communication, and future product strategy.
- Surface insurer-facing dashboards for engagement, coverage, and higher-risk cohort management.

## Success Metrics

- assessment completion rate
- repeat engagement rate
- movement programme completion rate
- risk-tier distribution over time
- customer satisfaction and perceived insurer value
- conversion into broader wellness participation

## Conclusion

HSBC Life ActiveShield helps reimagine insurance as a service that actively supports healthier living, not just financial recovery after adverse events. By combining preventive assessment, personalised intervention, and insurer insight, HSBC Life can build a more differentiated and future-ready health and wellness ecosystem.

This proposal is well aligned with the competition theme because it forges a new era of protection: one that is preventive, digital, measurable, and meaningfully embedded in customers' everyday lives.
