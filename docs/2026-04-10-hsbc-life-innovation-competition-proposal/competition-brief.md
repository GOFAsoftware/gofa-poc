# Competition Brief

Competition:
- HSBC Life Insurance Innovation Competition 2026

Official theme:
- Insurance Reimagined: Forging a New Era of Protection

What HSBC says it wants:
- Product and proposition design ideas
- Faster digitalisation and technology adoption
- Sustainability-oriented thinking
- Better customer experience for today and tomorrow

Eligibility confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Current full-time, non-final-year undergraduate students at a UGC-funded university
- 3 to 4 students per team
- Each student can only join one team
- Cross-university and cross-discipline teams are allowed

Submission route confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Submit application form and proposal by email
- Submission email: hsbclife.innovation@hsbc.com.hk

Important dates confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Information Sharing and Think Tank Session: 27 March 2026
- Proposal submission deadline: 24 April 2026, 23:59 HKT
- Announcement of finalist teams: week of 18 May 2026
- Mentor-mentee matching and preparatory meeting: week of 26 May 2026
- HSBC Life Insurance Expo 2026 and final booth presentation: 18 June 2026, TBC

Prize summary confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Champion: HKD 30,000
- 1st runner-up: HKD 20,000
- 2nd runner-up: HKD 10,000
- Most Popular Idea Award: HKD 10,000
- Participation gift for finalist teams not winning: HKD 1,000 vouchers

Career upside confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Winning teams receive fast-track access to final-round interview for the HSBC Hong Kong Student Work Placement, subject to eligibility
- Placement period noted by HSBC: January to June 2027

Proposal categories confirmed from HSBC public page:

1. GBA Market Penetration
- Cross-border product design across Hong Kong, Macau, and Mainland GBA cities
- Brand awareness and customer acquisition in GBA cities
- Cross-border healthcare, legacy planning, wealth management, and retirement planning

2. Silver Economy and Retirement Solution
- Better financial wellbeing and lifestyle support for retirees
- Longevity risk, healthcare costs, and changing family structure support
- Digital accessibility and engagement for older customers

3. Health & Wellness Ecosystem
- How insurers can move beyond claims payment into health and wellbeing partnership
- What partnerships with healthtech, fitness, and nutrition could expand value proposition
- How data from wearables and health apps can be used responsibly to improve outcomes

Judging criteria confirmed from HSBC public page:
- Innovation and use of technology
- Commercialisation potential
- Customer centricity
- ESG and sustainability
- Booth presentation

Additional judging note from search-result snippet that should be checked directly in the PDF before final submission:
- Innovation and use of technology appears to include a 20-point weighting

Best-fit category for GOFA:
- Health & Wellness Ecosystem

Why GOFA fits:
- Preventive assessment and wellness engagement already align with insurer-as-health-partner positioning
- Existing strengths map directly to fitness, healthy ageing, movement, data-driven engagement, and ongoing intervention
- GOFA can credibly combine risk identification, behaviour change, member engagement, and measurable insurer value
