# Recommended Proposal Direction

## Core Recommendation

Submit under:
- Health & Wellness Ecosystem

Working proposal title:
- HSBC Life ActiveShield: From Claims Payer to Daily Health Partner

Alternative titles:
- HSBC Life WellPath: A Preventive Health and Mobility Ecosystem for Families
- HSBC Life MoveCare: AI-Powered Prevention, Engagement, and Healthy Ageing
- HSBC Life Protect+Live: Turning Protection into Everyday Wellness Action

## Strategic Thesis

HSBC Life should not compete only on policy features. It should build a prevention-led ecosystem that helps members stay healthier for longer, especially in mobility decline, frailty risk, and healthy ageing. GOFA is a credible enabling layer because it already covers:
- functional and movement assessment
- fall-risk style screening and stratification
- wellness and exercise engagement
- older-adult accessibility use cases
- ongoing digital interaction rather than one-off claims moments

## Problem Statement

Traditional insurance interaction is too episodic:
- customers mostly engage at purchase, renewal, or claims
- health decline is detected too late
- wellness programmes often lack measurable outcomes
- older adults are underserved by generic health apps
- insurers struggle to show daily relevance

## Proposed Solution

Build an HSBC Life wellness ecosystem with four layers:

1. Preventive screening
- short digital onboarding
- mobility and balance screening
- self-reported lifestyle and health-risk factors
- optional wearable and health-app connections over time

2. Risk stratification
- identify low, medium, and high support needs
- flag mobility decline, frailty indicators, and healthy-ageing support needs
- translate assessment outputs into simple member-friendly next steps

3. Personalised intervention
- AI-guided movement routines
- healthy-ageing programmes for older members
- rehabilitation-style progression for higher-risk cohorts
- family-friendly nudges, reminders, and habit loops

4. Insurer intelligence layer
- portfolio risk mix
- assessment coverage
- engagement trends
- early intervention opportunities
- partner reporting and product-design insight

## Why HSBC Life Should Care

Business value:
- stronger customer engagement between policy events
- differentiated insurer brand around prevention and care
- better retention through ongoing member value
- better product and proposition insight from behavioural data
- potential claims reduction and earlier intervention in high-cost deterioration journeys

Customer value:
- clearer understanding of current health status
- practical next actions instead of generic wellness content
- accessible digital engagement for older adults
- more relevant support for families and caregivers

## What Makes The Proposal Competitive

To score well, the proposal should clearly show:

Innovation and use of technology:
- AI-assisted movement and wellness journeys
- digital assessment to intervention loop
- privacy-conscious use of app and wearable inputs

Commercialisation potential:
- simple rollout path: pilot, targeted segment, insurer integration, portfolio expansion
- multiple member segments: adults, retirees, family caregivers, healthy-ageing users

Customer centricity:
- easy onboarding
- plain-language feedback
- accessible design for older customers
- intervention matched to readiness and risk

ESG and sustainability:
- supports healthy ageing and preventive care
- improves inclusivity and access
- encourages proactive health behaviour rather than late-stage intervention only

Booth presentation:
- demoable customer journey
- before/after member story
- dashboard view for HSBC stakeholders

## Proposed Demo Narrative

Member journey to present:
- member joins via HSBC Life wellness programme
- completes a 5-minute preventive assessment
- receives a simple status and recommended next step
- starts a tailored movement plan
- shows progress and reassessment outcome after several weeks
- HSBC Life dashboard shows aggregate engagement and risk movement

## Recommended Final Deliverable Angle

Do not pitch GOFA as only a vendor tool.

Pitch it as:
- a new HSBC Life wellness proposition
- enabled by digital assessment, AI-guided engagement, and preventive health intelligence

That framing is stronger because it aligns with how HSBC describes product, proposition, digitalisation, and customer experience transformation.
